Piston, Connecting Rod, and Bearing Replacement

Special Tools

EN-45059 Torque Angle Sensor Kit

For equivalent regional tools, refer to Special Tools .

Removal Procedure
  1. Open hood.
  2. Remove the cylinder head. Refer to  Cylinder Head Replacement .
  3. Remove the oil pan. Refer to  Oil Pan Replacement .
  4. Raise the vehicle by its full height. Refer to Lifting and Jacking the Vehicle .
  5. Install the crankshaft pulley bolt.
  6. Set the pistons 1 and 4 to TDC in direction of engine rotation.


    7. Note: Note cylinder sequence.

  7. Mark the connecting rod with the connecting rod bearing cover (1).


  8. Remove the 4 connecting rod bearing caps bolts (2) of cylinder 1 and 4.
  9. Remove the connecting rod bearing caps (1) and the connecting rod bearing (3).
  10. Lower the vehicle by its full height.


  11. Push the pistons 1 and 4 (1) out of the cylinder bore (2).

    12. Note: The shear surfaces of the con-rod and the con-rod bearing cover form a unique fit and must not be swapped or damaged. Do not lay down on the shear surfaces.

  12. Remove the pistons 1 and 4 (1).
  13. Turn crankshaft on crankshaft balancer through 180° in direction of rotation of engine.


    14. Note: Note cylinder sequence.

  14. Mark the connecting rod with the connecting rod bearing cover (1).
  15. Raise the vehicle by its full height.


  16. Remove the 4 connecting rod bearing caps bolts (2) of cylinder 2 and 3.
  17. Remove the connecting rod bearing caps (1) and the connecting rod bearing (3).
  18. Lower the vehicle by its full height.


  19. Push the pistons 2 and 3 (1) out of the cylinder bore (2).

    20. Note: The shear surfaces of the con-rod and the con-rod bearing cover form a unique fit and must not be swapped or damaged. Do not lay down on the shear surfaces.

  20. Remove the pistons 2 and 3 (1).


    21. Note: Observe correct fitting position, observe alignment.

  21. Remove the connecting rod bearing (1, 2).
  22. Check the components. Refer to Piston, Connecting Rod, and Bearing Cleaning and Inspection .

Installation procedure


    Note: Lubricate the connection rod bearing with NEW engine oil.

  1. Install the connecting rod bearing (1, 2).


  2. Set piston ring joints.
    3. • Piston compression upper ring in position (1).
    • Piston compression lower ring in position (4).
    • Interim ring of the piston oil ring rail in position (5), steel band rings of the piston oil ring rail in position (2) and/or (3).


  3. Lubricate the piston rings, piston (1), inner cylinder bore surface and EN-48249 compressor  (2) with clean engine oil.

    4. Note: Arrow on piston head points to engine timing side, bead on connecting rod points to transmission side of engine.



  4. Push the pistons 2 and 3 (1) into the cylinder bore (2).
  5. Raise the vehicle by its full height.


  6. Install the connecting rod bearing caps (1) and the connecting rod bearing (3).
  7. Install 4 NEW connecting rod bearing caps bolts (2) of cylinder 2 and 3 and tighten in 3 passes:

    8. Caution: Refer to Fastener Caution in the Preface section.

    7.1. in first pass tighten to  25N·m (19 lb ft)
    7.2. in second pass tighten to  30° using the EN-45059 angle sensor
  8. Turn crankshaft on crankshaft balancer through 180° in direction of rotation of engine.
  9. Lower the vehicle by its full height.


  10. Push the pistons 1 and 4 (1) into the cylinder bore (2).
  11. Raise the vehicle by its full height.


  12. Install the connecting rod bearing caps (1) and the connecting rod bearing (3).
  13. Install the 4 connecting rod bearing caps bolts (2) of cylinder 1 and 4 and tighten in 3 passes:
    14. 13.1. in first pass tighten to  25N·m (19 lb ft)
    13.2. in second pass tighten to  30° using the EN-45059 angle sensor
  14. Set the crankshaft in direction of engine rotation to 60° before TDC.
  15. Remove the crankshaft pulley bolt.
  16. Install the oil pan. Refer to  Oil Pan Replacement
  17. Install the cylinder head. Refer to  Cylinder Head Replacement
  18. Close hood.
