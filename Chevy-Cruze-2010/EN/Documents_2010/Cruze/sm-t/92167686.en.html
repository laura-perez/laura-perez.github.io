Circuit/System Description

The body control module (BCM) controls the door lock, passenger door unlock and driver door unlock circuits. Voltage is applied to the appropriate circuit when the BCM senses a door lock/unlock switch activation or a keyless entry lock or unlock command.

Conditions for Running the DTC

The system voltage is 9-18 V.

Conditions for Setting the DTC

B3125 01

The BCM detects a short to battery on the driver door unlock control circuit.

B3125 02

The BCM detects a short to ground on the driver door unlock control circuit.

B3125 04

The BCM detects an open circuit on the driver door unlock control circuit.

B3130 01

The BCM detects a short to battery on the unlock door control circuit.

B3130 02

The BCM detects a short to ground on the unlock door control circuit.

B3130 04

The BCM detects an open circuit on the unlock door control circuit.

B3135 01

The BCM detects a short to battery on the door lock control circuit.

B3135 02

The BCM detects a short to ground on the door lock control circuit.

B3135 04

The BCM detects an open circuit on the door lock control circuit.

Action Taken When the DTC Sets

The power door locks will be inoperative.

Conditions for Clearing the DTC
    • This DTC will be current for as long as the fault is present.
    • When the fault is no longer present, the DTC will be a history DTC.
    • A history DTC will clear after 50 ignition cycles.

Circuit/System Verification

Command the All Door Lock, Driver Door Unlock and Passenger Door(s) Unlock with the scan tool, the doors should perform the lock and unlock function.

Circuit/System Testing
  1. Ignition OFF, disconnect the malfunctioning door latch connector.
  2. Connect a test lamp between the appropriate door lock control circuit and the unlock control circuit.
    3. • A23D Door Latch Assembly - Driver terminals 2 and 3.
    • A23P Door Latch Assembly - Passenger terminals 7 and 8.
    • A23LR Door Latch Assembly - Left Rear terminals 2 and 3.
    • A23RR Door Latch Assembly - Right Rear terminals 7 and 8.
  3. Command the doors to Unlock with a scan tool. The test lamp should briefly illuminate when commanding the Unlock state.
    4. ⇒If the test lamp is always OFF, test the control circuit for a short to voltage or an open/high resistance. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K9 Body Control Module.
    ⇒ If the test lamp is always ON, test the control circuit for a short to ground. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K9 Body Control Module.
  4. If all circuits test normal, test or replace the door latch.

Component Testing

Door Latch Test

  1. Ignition OFF, disconnect the harness connector at the appropriate door lock assembly.
  2. Install a 25 A fused jumper wire between the lock control terminal and B+. Momentarily install a jumper wire between the unlock control terminal and ground. The door lock should perform the lock/unlock function.
    3. ⇒If the function does not perform as specified, replace the door lock.
  3. Reverse the jumper wires; the appropriate door lock should perform the unlock/lock function.
    4. ⇒If the function does not perform as specified, replace the door lock.
