Front Brake Shield Replacement

Special Tools

EN-45059 Torque Angle Sensor Kit

For equivalent regional tools, refer to Special Tools

Removal Procedure
  1. Raise and suitably support the vehicle. Refer to Lifting and Jacking the Vehicle .
  2. Remove the brake rotor. Refer to Front Brake Rotor Replacement .


    3. Caution: Support the brake caliper with heavy mechanic wire, or equivalent, whenever it is separated from its mount and the hydraulic flexible brake hose is still connected. Failure to support the caliper in this manner will cause the flexible brake hose to bear the weight of the caliper, which may cause damage to the brake hose and in turn may cause a brake fluid leak.

  3. Remove the wheel speed sensor bolt.
  4. Remove the wheel speed sensor.
  5. Remove the wheel speed sensor wiring harness from the steering knuckle.
  6. Loosen the wheel drive shaft from the wheel bearing/hub. Refer to Front Wheel Drive Shaft Replacement - Right Side .


  7. Remove and DISCARD the wheel bearing/hub mounting bolts (1).


  8. Remove the wheel bearing/hub and front brake shield from the steering knuckle.

Installation Procedure


  1. Position the front brake shield and wheel bearing/hub assembly in the steering knuckle.

    2. Caution: Refer to Fastener Caution in the Preface section.



    Caution: This is a self-retaining fastener joint that does not require thread locking compounds. Do not attempt to clean the threads with a standard tap. If a standard tap is used, damage to the joint threads will occur.

  2. Install the NEW wheel bearing/hub mounting bolts (1).
  3. Tighten the bearing/hub bolts (1) in 3 passes, using the EN-45059 kit
    4. 3.1. First pass to  90 N·m (67 lb ft)
    3.2. Second pass to  60°
    3.3. Third pass to  15°


  4. Install the wheel speed sensor wiring harness to the steering knuckle.
  5. Install the wheel speed sensor.
  6. Install the wheel speed sensor bolt. and tighten to  6 N·m (53 lb in).
  7. Install the brake rotor. Refer to Front Brake Rotor Replacement .
  8. Install the wheel drive shaft retaining nut and washer. Refer to Front Wheel Drive Shaft Replacement - Right Side .
  9. Remove the support and lower the vehicle.
