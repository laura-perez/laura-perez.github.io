Circuit/System Description

Ground is applied at all times to the indicator/multifunction switch. The indicator lamps may only be activated with the ignition switch in the ON or START position. When the indicator/multifunction switch is placed in either the TURN RIGHT or TURN LEFT position, ground is applied to the body control module (BCM) through either the right turn or left indicator switch signal circuit. The BCM then applies a pulsating voltage to the front and rear indicator lamps through there respective voltage supply circuits. When a indicator request is received by the BCM, a serial data message is sent to the instrument cluster requesting the respective indicator indicator be pulsed ON and OFF.

Circuit/System Verification

Ignition ON, observe the scan tool Right Indicator Switch parameter and Left Indicator Switch parameter while switching the S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch between the left and right positions. The readings should toggle between ACTIVE and INACTIVE.

⇒If the readings do not change between the commanded states, refer to Indicator Switch Malfunction.
⇒If the readings change between the commanded states and the instrument cluster indicator indicators are inoperative, refer to Indicator Indicators Malfunction.

Circuit/System Testing

Indicator Switch Malfunction

  1. Ignition OFF, disconnect the harness connector at the S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch.
  2. Ignition OFF, verify that a test lamp illuminates between B+ and the ground circuit terminal 3.
    3. ⇒If the test lamp does not illuminate, test the ground circuit for an open/high resistance.
  3. Ignition ON, verify the scan tool Left Indicator Switch parameter is INACTIVE.
    4. ⇒If not the specified value, test the signal circuit terminal 1 for a short to ground. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K9 Body Control Module (BCM).
  4. Verify the scan tool Right Indicator Switch parameter is INACTIVE.
    5. ⇒If not the specified value, test the signal circuit terminal 7 for a short to ground. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K9 Body Control Module (BCM).
  5. Install a 3 A fused jumper wire between the signal circuit terminal 1 and ground. Verify the scan tool Left Indicator Switch parameter is ACTIVE.
    6. ⇒If not the specified value, test the signal circuit for a short to voltage or an open/high resistance. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K9 Body Control Module (BCM).
  6. Install a 3 A fused jumper wire between the signal circuit terminal 7 and ground. Verify the scan tool Right Indicator Switch parameter is ACTIVE.
    7. ⇒If not the specified value, test the signal circuit for a short to voltage or an open/high resistance. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K9 Body Control Module (BCM).
  7. If all circuits test normal, test or replace the S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch.

Indicator Lamps Malfunction

  1. Ignition OFF, disconnect the harness connector at the inoperative indicator lamp.
  2. Ignition OFF, exterior lamps OFF, test for less than 5.0 Ω between the appropriate ground circuit terminal listed below and ground.
    3. • E4N Park/Indicator Lamp - Left ground circuit terminal 2
    • E4P Park/Indicator Lamp - Right ground circuit terminal 2
    • E4LR Indicator Lamp - Left Rear ground circuit terminal 3
    • E4RR Indicator Lamp - Right Rear ground circuit terminal 3
    • E4Y Indicator Repeater Lamp - Left ground circuit terminal 2
    • E4Z Indicator Repeater Lamp - Right ground circuit terminal 2
    ⇒If greater than the specified range, test the ground circuit for an open/high resistance.
  3. Connect a test lamp between the appropriate control circuit terminal listed below and ground.
    4. • E4N Park/Indicator Lamp - Left control circuit terminal 1
    • E4P Park/Indicator Lamp - Right control circuit terminal 1
    • E4LR Indicator Lamp - Left Rear control circuit terminal 2
    • E4RR Indicator Lamp - Right Rear control circuit terminal 2
    • E4Y Indicator Repeater Lamp - Left control circuit terminal 1
    • E4Z Indicator Repeater Lamp - Right control circuit terminal 1
  4. Command with the appropriate Indicator Lamp ON and OFF with a scan tool. The test lamp should turn ON and OFF when changing between the commanded states.
    5. ⇒If the test lamp is always ON, test the control circuit for a short to voltage. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K9 Body Control Module (BCM).
    ⇒If the test lamp is always OFF, test the control circuit for a short to ground or an open/high resistance. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K9 Body Control Module (BCM).
  5. If all circuits test normal, replace the appropriate indicator lamp assembly.

Indicator Indicators Malfunction

Ignition ON, command the All Indicators test ON and OFF with the scan tool, verify both indicator indicators changes between turn ON and OFF.

⇒If both indicator indicators changes between illuminate and not illuminate during the test, replace the K9 Body Control Module (BCM).
⇒If one or both indicator indicators do not illuminate during the test or remain illuminated at all times, replace the P16 Instrument Cluster.

Component Testing

Static Test

Indicator Switch

  1. Ignition OFF, disconnect the harness connector at the S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch.
  2. Remove S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch.
  3. Test for greater than 500 kΩ between signal terminal 1 and ground terminal 3.
    4. ⇒If less than the specified value, replace the S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch.
  4. Test for greater than 500 kΩ between signal terminal 7 and ground terminal 3.
    5. ⇒If less than the specified value, replace the S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch.
  5. Push the S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch turn left. Test for less than 5 Ω between signal terminal 1 and ground terminal 3.
    6. ⇒If greater than the specified value, replace the S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch.
  6. Push the S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch turn right. Test for less than 5 Ω between signal terminal 7 and ground terminal 3.
    7. ⇒If greater than the specified value, replace the S78 Indicator/Multifunction Switch.
