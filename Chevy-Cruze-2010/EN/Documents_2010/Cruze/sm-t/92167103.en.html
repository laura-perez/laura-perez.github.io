Check Connecting Rod Bearing Clearance (with Plastigage)


    Note: Do not rotate the crankshaft.

  1. Lay on a suitable plastigage (flexible plastic thread) over the entire width of the connecting rod bearing journal.

    2. Caution: Refer to Fastener Caution in the Preface section.

  2. Install the connecting rod bearing cap.
  3. Tighten the 2 bolts in three steps:
    4. 3.1. In first pass tighten to  25 N·m (19 lb ft)
    3.2. In second pass tighten to  90 degrees using the EN-45059 angle sensor
  4. Remove the con-rod bearing cap bolts.
  5. Remove the connecting rod bearing cap.


    6. Note: When reading the value, do not confuse millimeters and inches on the measuring scale.

  6. Measure the con-rod bearing play.
    7. • Compare the width of the flattened plastic thread with the measuring scale.
    • Permissible connecting rod bearing play: 0.09-0.32 mm (0.004-0.014 lb in).

Check Connecting Rod Bearing Clearance (With Micrometer Gage Internal Measuring Device)


  1. Measure the connecting rod bearing diameters at 3 points A, B and C with the internal measuring device.
  2. Work out the average connecting rod diameter.

    3. Formula: A + B + C /3.



  3. Measure at points I and II with the micrometer gauge.


  4. Measure the connecting rod bearing journal diameter at 2 points.

    5. Formula: I + II/2.

  5. Work out the average connecting rod bearing journal diameter.
  6. Determine the connecting rod bearing play.

    7. Calculation formula: average connecting rod bearing diameter -- average connecting rod bearing journal diameter.

  7. Perform the target/actual comparison.

    8. Permissible connecting rod bearing play: 0.09-0.32 mm (0.004-0.014 lb in).
