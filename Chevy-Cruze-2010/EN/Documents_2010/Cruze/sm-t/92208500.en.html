Engine Block Cleaning and Inspection

Special Tools

EN-8087 Cylinder Gauge

For equivalent regional tools, refer to Special Tools .



  1. Clean the sealing material from the gasket mating surfaces (2).
  2. Clean the engine block (1) and lower crankcase in a cleaning tank with solvent appropriate for aluminum. Refer to Adhesives, Fluids, Lubricants, and Sealers for the recommended solvent.
  3. Flush the engine block with clean water or steam.
  4. Clean the oil passages.
  5. Clean the blind holes.
  6. Spray the cylinder bores and the machined surfaces with engine oil.
  7. Inspect the threaded holes. Clean the threaded holes with a rifle brush. If necessary, drill out the holes and install thread inserts. Refer to Thread Inserts .


    8. Note: Do not attempt to machine the lower crankcase to engine block surfaces.



  8. Engine block for sag in length and width on the sealing surfaces and for distortion along the diagonals.

    9. If the deck surface is out of specification, replace the block. Do not machine the block.



  9. Clean the sealing material from the gasket mating surfaces on the lower crankcase oil pan side.


  10. Inspect the crankshaft main bearing bores. Use the EN-8087 gauge  (1) to measure the bearing bore concentricity and alignment.
  11. Replace the engine block and bed plate if the crankshaft bearing bores are out of specification.


  12. Inspect the cylinder bores using the EN-8087 gauge  (1). Inspect for the following items:
    13. • Wear
    • Taper
    • Runout
    • Ridging
  13. Refer to Engine Mechanical Specifications .
  14. If the cylinder bores are out of specification, replace the engine block.
