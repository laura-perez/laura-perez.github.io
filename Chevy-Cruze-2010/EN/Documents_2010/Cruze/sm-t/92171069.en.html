Engine Oil and Oil Filter Replacement

Removal Procedure
  1. Open the hood.
  2. Place a collecting basin underneath.


  3. Remove the oil filter cap (1).
  4. Remove the oil filter cap seal (2)

    5. Caution: This engine uses a special high performance oil filter. Use of any other filter may lead to filter failure and/or severe engine damage.

  5. Remove and properly dispose of the oil filter insert  (3).
  6. Raise the vehicle by its full height. Refer to Lifting and Jacking the Vehicle .
  7. Remove the oil drain bolt.
  8. Drain the engine oil into collecting basin.

Installation Procedure
  1. Clean the oil drain bolt thread and the thread in the oil pan.
  2. Install a NEW seal to the oil drain bolt.

    3. Caution: Refer to Fastener Caution in the Preface section.

  3. Install the oil drain bolt to the oil pan and tighten to  14 N·m (124 lb in).
  4. Lower the vehicle by its full height.


  5. Install the NEW oil filter insert (3).

    6. Note: Coat the seal ring with NEW engine oil.

  6. Install the NEW oil filter cap seal (2)

    7. Caution: Over torquing the oil filter cap may cause damage to the oil filter cap resulting in an oil leak.

  7. Install the oil filter cap (1) and tighten to  25 N·m (18 lb ft).

    8. Caution: Using engine oils of any viscosity other than those viscosities recommended could result in engine damage.

    Note: Use specified volume of engine oil with the specified viscosity class.

    Note: Start the engine and allow it to run until the oil pressure control indicator goes off.

    Note: Inspect the engine oil level.

  8. Fill in NEW engine oil.
  9. Close the hood
  10. Reset the service interval indicator.
