Piston, Connecting Rod, and Bearing Removal

  1. Install the crankshaft pulley bolt.
  2. Set the pistons 1 and 4 to BDC in direction of engine rotation.


    3. Note: Note cylinder sequence.

  3. Mark the connecting rod with the connecting rod bearing cover (1).


  4. Remove the 4 connecting rod bearing caps bolts (2) of cylinder 1 and 4.
  5. Remove the connecting rod bearing caps (1) and the connecting rod bearing (3).
  6. Rotate the engine 180°.


  7. Push the pistons 1 and 4 (1) out of the cylinder bore (2).

    8. Note: The shear surfaces of the connecting rod and the connecting rod bearing cover form a unique fit and must not be swapped or damaged. Do not lay down on the shear surfaces.

  8. Remove the pistons 1 and 4 (1).
  9. Turn crankshaft on crankshaft balancer through 180° in direction of rotation of engine.


    10. Note: Note cylinder sequence.

  10. Mark the connecting rod with the connecting rod bearing cover (1).
  11. Rotate the engine 180°.


  12. Remove the 4 connecting rod bearing caps bolts (2) of cylinder 2 and 3.
  13. Remove the connecting rod bearing caps (1) and the connecting rod bearing (3).
  14. Rotate the engine 180°.


  15. Push the pistons 2 and 3 (1) out of the cylinder bore (2).

    16. Note: The shear surfaces of the connecting rod and the connecting rod bearing cover form a unique fit and must not be swapped or damaged. Do not lay down on the shear surfaces.

  16. Remove the pistons 2 and 3 (1).


    17. Note: Observe correct fitting position, observe alignment.

  17. Remove the connecting rod bearing (1, 2).
  18. Check the components. Refer to Piston, Connecting Rod, and Bearing Cleaning and Inspection .
