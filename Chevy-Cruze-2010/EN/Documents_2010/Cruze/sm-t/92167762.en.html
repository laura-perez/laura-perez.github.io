Stabilizer Shaft Insulator Replacement

Special Tools

EN 45059 Torque Angle Sensor Kit

For equivalent regional tools, refer to Special Tools .

Removal Procedure
  1. Raise and support the vehicle. Refer to Lifting and Jacking the Vehicle .
  2. Remove the front tire and wheel assemblies. Refer to Tire and Wheel Removal and Installation .
  3. Disconnect the stabilizer links from the stabilizer shaft. Refer to Stabilizer Shaft Link Replacement .
  4. Remove the frame to body bolts. Refer to Drivetrain and Front Suspension Frame Replacement .
  5. Lower the rear of the frame in order to gain clearance to the stabilizer shaft.


    6. Note: In order to assure a correct installation of the insulators ONLY remove and install the insulators side by side! Dismantled MUST BE discarded.

  6. Remove and discard the two front stabilizer shaft insulator clamp bolts (1).
  7. Remove the shaft insulator clamp (2).

    8. Bend up clamp (2) slightly.



  8. Mark the location of the insulator (1) to the stabilizer shaft (2).
  9. Remove insulator (1) from stabilizer shaft (2).

Installation Procedure


  1. Install NEW stabilizer shaft insulators (1) to stabilizer shaft (2) so that he slit in the insulator is facing the rear of the vehicle.


  2. Install the insulator clamp to the insulator:
    3. 2.1. Position the clamp over on the insulator.
    2.2. Use two M8 bolts with 40 mm (1.57 in) length.
    2.3. Tighten bolts by turns until clamp slips over insulator.


  3. Check proper fitting of clamp and insulator.

    4. Retainer (1) MUST seat centrically in the hole of the clamp.

    Caution: Refer to Fastener Caution in the Preface section.



  4. Install the NEW stabilizer shaft bolts (1) and tighten the NEW stabilizer shaft bolts to  22 N·m (17 lb ft) + 40° using the EN 45059 kit .
  5. Install the frame to body bolts. Refer to Drivetrain and Front Suspension Frame Replacement .
  6. Connect the stabilizer links to the stabilizer shaft. Refer to Stabilizer Shaft Link Replacement .
  7. Install the front tire and wheel assemblies. Refer to Tire and Wheel Removal and Installation .
  8. Lower the vehicle.
