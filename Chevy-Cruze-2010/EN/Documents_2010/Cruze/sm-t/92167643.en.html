Circuit/System Description

The keyless entry control module and body control module (BCM) control the output functions of the power door catches. These output functions allow the keyless entry control module and BCM to command the power door catch motors to Uncatch.

When the keyless entry control module receives an input from one of the door handle switches, it checks the Lock/Unlock status of the door. If the status of the door is Lock, the keyless entry control module applies the ground for the appropriate uncatch circuit to the BCM and the BCM will unlock the door catch.

Conditions for Running the DTC

The system voltage is 9-18 V.

Conditions for Setting the DTC

B251A 01

The BCM detects a short to battery on the driver door uncatch control circuit.

B251A 02

The BCM detects a short to ground on the driver door uncatch control circuit.

B251A 04

The BCM detects an open circuit on the driver door uncatch control circuit.

B251B 01

The BCM detects a short to battery on the passenger door uncatch control circuit.

B251B 02

The BCM detects a short to ground on the passenger door uncatch control circuit.

B251B 04

The BCM detects an open circuit on the passenger door uncatch control circuit.

Action Taken When the DTC Sets

The power door catches will be inoperative

Conditions for Clearing the DTC
    • This DTC will be current for as long as the fault is present.
    • When the fault is no longer present, the DTC will be a history DTC.
    • A history DTC will clear after 50 ignition cycles.

Circuit/System Verification

Command the driver door and passenger door lock actuators to Unlock with the key fob, the doors should perform the Unlock function.

Circuit/System Testing
  1. Ignition OFF, disconnect the harness connector X1 at the K9 Body Control Module (BCM).
  2. Ignition ON, test for B+ between the appropriate door uncatch control circuit and B+.
    3. • K9 Body Control Module (BCM) terminal 5
    • K9 Body Control Module (BCM) terminal 17
    ⇒If less than the specified range, test the circuit for an open/high resistance. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K77 Remote Control Door Lock Receiver (RCDLR).
  3. Ignition ON, test for less than 1 V between the appropriate door uncatch control circuit and ground.
    4. • K9 Body Control Module (BCM) terminal 5
    • K9 Body Control Module (BCM) terminal 17
    ⇒If greater than the specified range, test the circuit for a short to voltage. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K77 Remote Control Door Lock Receiver (RCDLR).
    ⇒If less than the specified range, test the circuit for an open/high resistance. If the circuit tests normal, replace the K77 Remote Control Door Lock Receiver (RCDLR).
  4. If all circuits test normal, test or replace the K9 Body Control Module (BCM).
